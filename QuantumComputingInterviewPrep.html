Quantum Mechanics Fundamentals

This section covers the foundational principles of quantum mechanics, from the historical experiments that revealed the breakdown of classical physics to the core mathematical framework used to describe quantum systems. Understanding these concepts is essential for grasping how quantum computers work at a fundamental level.

Quantum Computing Core Concepts

Here, we transition from pure theory to computation. This section introduces the building blocks of quantum computers: the qubit and the quantum logic gates. We'll explore the unique properties of superposition and entanglement that give quantum computers their power, and visualize how these concepts are represented.

Quantum Hardware & Tools

A central topic for your role. This section provides an overview of the physical systems used to build qubits. We compare different hardware modalities, discuss the critical challenges of coherence and quantum error correction, and touch upon the classical hardware and software tools required to control and measure these delicate quantum systems.

Quantum Algorithms & QML

Finally, we explore how to use quantum computers. This section outlines famous quantum algorithms that promise speedups over their classical counterparts. We'll also look at the emerging field of Quantum Machine Learning (QML), a key application area for near-term quantum devices and a focus of current research.
