Is the security of quantum cryptography guaranteed by the laws of physics?

An interactive analysis of the paper by

Daniel J. Bernstein

Abstract

It is often claimed that the security of theoretical quantum key distribution (QKD) is guaranteed by the laws of physics. However, this claim is content-free if the underlying definition of theoretical QKD is not actually compatible with the laws of physics. This paper observes that (1) the laws of physics pose serious obstacles to the security of QKD and (2) these laws are ignored in all QKD "security proofs".

The Common Claim

This section highlights the prevailing belief about Quantum Key Distribution (QKD). For decades, QKD has been presented as the ultimate security solution, with its safety guaranteed not by mathematical complexity, but by the fundamental laws of nature.

"QKD offers the ultimate security of the inviolability of a law of Nature for key distribution."

"Quantum cryptography differs from conventional cryptography in that the data are kept secret by the properties of quantum mechanics, rather than the conjectured difficulty of computing certain functions."

The core idea is simple: if an eavesdropper (Eve) tries to measure the quantum state sent from Alice to Bob, the very act of measurement disturbs the state, revealing her presence. This, it is claimed, provides "perfect security."

The "Proof" Model

To "prove" QKD is secure, scientists use a theoretical model. This model simplifies the world to make the mathematics solvable. This section illustrates that simplified model and highlights the critical assumptions it makes. The paper's core argument is that these assumptions are not physically realistic.

Key Assumptions of the "Proof":

  • The system is perfectly isolated from the rest of the universe.
  • Eve's power is limited only by the laws of quantum mechanics applied to the quantum channel itself.
  • The devices (Alice, Bob) are perfect, abstract "black boxes" that do not leak any other information.
  • The "laws of physics" in the model are only the parts of quantum mechanics being used, not all known physics.

The Physical Reality: Ignored Obstacles

This is the central argument of the paper. The "security proofs" are only valid within their own simplified model. They fail by ignoring other, equally real laws of physics that create massive security holes. Click the tabs below to explore these "ignored obstacles."

Side-Channel Attacks

The Physics: Physical devices are not abstract boxes. They are objects in the real world that interact with their environment. They consume power, make faint sounds, and radiate heat and electromagnetic signals as they operate.

The Flaw: QKD "security proofs" completely ignore this. They assume the *only* information leak is through the quantum channel. An attacker (Eve) doesn't need to measure the photon; she can just measure the power consumption of Alice's device or the electromagnetic radiation from Bob's detector. These "side channels" can leak the *entire secret key*, and they are not protected by quantum mechanics at all.

Information Flow

The Physics: Information cannot travel faster than the speed of light. This fundamental limit (relativity) governs how and when information is available to an attacker.

The Flaw: Many QKD security proofs are "unphysical" in their treatment of time and information. They might mathematically combine information from different points in spacetime (e.g., Alice's operation and Bob's later operation) in a way that assumes an attacker could access both simultaneously. A physically realistic proof must respect the causal structure of spacetime‚Äîan attacker can only act on the information available to them *at their location and time*.

Electromagnetism

The Physics: This is the most common and powerful side channel. Any flow of electricity (which QKD devices are full of) creates an electromagnetic field. These fields travel outwards at the speed of light, carrying detailed information about the electrical signals that created them. The Flaw: The QKD "proof" model assumes the device is a "black box." In reality, an attacker can place an antenna near the QKD device and read the secret key directly from its electromagnetic emissions. This is a classic, well-understood attack (like TEMPEST) from non-quantum cryptography, and the "laws of physics" (Maxwell's equations) *guarantee* this information leak, while QKD proofs ignore it.

Gravity

The Physics: Every object with mass (like a QKD device and its components) exerts a gravitational force. This force changes minutely as components move or as the device processes data (mass-energy equivalence). The Flaw: While extraordinarily difficult to measure, this is another physical side channel that is *guaranteed* to exist by the laws of physics (General Relativity). A complete "security proof" that claims to be based on *all* laws of physics would have to account for an attacker using a hyper-sensitive gravitational wave detector to "listen" to the device. The fact that this is ignored shows how selective the "proofs" are.

The Holographic Principle

The Physics: This is a more advanced concept from theoretical physics. It suggests that all the information contained within a volume of space (like the room holding the QKD device) can be fully described by the information on the *boundary* of that space (the walls of the room). The Flaw: If this principle is true, it has profound security implications. An attacker (Eve) might not need to enter the room or even measure the device itself. By completely monitoring the *boundary* (the walls), she could, in theory, reconstruct *all* information inside, including the secret key. No QKD proof even begins to consider this physical possibility.

Conclusion: A "Content-Free" Claim

This interactive analysis brings us to the paper's final, powerful conclusion. The claim that "QKD security is guaranteed by the laws of physics" is misleading at best.

The "security proofs" for QKD are not based on the *complete* laws of physics. They are based on a tiny, idealized subset of quantum mechanics.


These proofs systematically ignore other, equally fundamental laws (like electromagnetism, relativity, and gravity) which actively create vulnerabilities (side channels) that can completely break the system.


Therefore, the claim is "content-free" because the very laws of physics it relies on are the same laws that are being ignored.
